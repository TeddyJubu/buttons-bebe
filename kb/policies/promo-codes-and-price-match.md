## Price drop after purchase — no price adjustment

We do **not** offer price adjustments after an order is placed. If an item a
customer bought later drops in price or goes on sale, the order stands at the price
paid — decline kindly and do **not** retroactively match the lower price. (Confirmed
in `order-changes-and-cancellations.md`.) Do not promise a "price-match refund" in a
draft.

## Forgot to apply a promo code at checkout

If a customer forgot to apply a current promo code, handle it **case by case** and
only while the order has **not shipped** — verify the code is valid and still within
its promotion window first. Because applying it after the fact would mean refunding a
difference, and **refunds are escalation-only**, surface it to a human rather than
promising the refund in an automated draft. If the code is **expired or the sale has
ended**, it is not applied retroactively (flash-sale codes expire when the sale ends
and are not extended) — apologize.

## Promo codes on sale items

In most cases a promo code **cannot be combined** with an existing sale discount, and
some codes exclude sale items. Check the specific promotion's terms (or confirm with
the team) before telling a customer a code will stack on an already-discounted item.

## Discounts and the final-sale threshold

An item discounted **20% or more** is **final sale** (no return or exchange by mail).
If a customer asks whether their discounted order is returnable, check the discount
level on the order. See `return-windows-and-refund-tiers.md`.
