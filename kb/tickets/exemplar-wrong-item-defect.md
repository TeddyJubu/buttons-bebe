## Customer situation

Customer received an item that doesn't match the listing or arrived defective
(e.g. a footie listed as "open feet" arrived closed-feet, or two sizes arrived
mis-sized versus each other). They include or are asked for a photo.

## How it was handled

An authorized human agent recognized this as the vendor's/our error (not a buyer
return), apologized, provided an approved prepaid return label, and contacted the
brand when needed. This is historical context only. The AI must not create or
provide a label, contact a brand, create a replacement, issue credit, or claim
that any of those actions happened.

## Safe AI draft while staff reviews

> Hi — so sorry about that, it looks like a mistake on the manufacturer's end.
> Thanks for sending the photo. Our team is reviewing the order and will follow
> up with the approved next step.

Only a human may add a return label or confirm a replacement or store credit after
the relevant action is authorized and completed.

## Why this is the model

- Treats a defect as our responsibility — no final-sale language.
- Safe AI step: collect the photo and hand the decision to authorized staff.
- **Does not promise or perform a refund, label, replacement, or credit** — each
  operational action belongs to a human.
