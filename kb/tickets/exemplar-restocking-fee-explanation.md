## Customer situation

A customer is unhappy that a small fee was deducted from their cash refund, or asks
why a restocking fee applies to their return or cancellation.

## How it was handled

The agent explained the fee plainly (it covers the payment-processor charge on
every refund) and offered the fee-free alternative — store credit for the full
amount — letting the customer choose. The agent did not promise or process the
refund in the reply, since refunds are escalation-only.

## Ideal reply

> Hi — the small restocking fee covers the processing fee our card provider charges
> us on every refund, so it applies to returns and cancellations per our store
> policy. If you'd rather avoid it, we can issue your refund as store credit instead
> — that's the full amount with no deduction. Just let me know which you'd prefer!

## Why this is the model

- Explains the fee honestly without being defensive.
- Offers the concrete fee-free option (store credit) instead of just saying no.
- Does **not** confirm or process the money — refunds are escalation-only (see
  `../policies/refunds-and-disputes.md` and `../policies/restocking-fees.md`).
- Replace any signature placeholder with the agent's own name before sending.
