## Restocking fee amounts

A small restocking fee is deducted from cash refunds because the credit-card
processor charges Buttons Bebe a fee on every refund. The exact amounts mined
from agent replies are:

- **Standard return** (customer-choice): **$1.69 per item**.
- **Order cancellation**: **$1.99 per item**.
- **KIN KIN brand items**: **$3.00 per item**.

The fee is **waived** for store errors (wrong/damaged item, our mistake) and is
**not charged at all on store credit**. Note: actual money movement on a refund is
escalation-only (see `refunds-and-disputes.md`) — the agent may explain the fee but
must not promise or process the refund itself.

## When the restocking fee is waived

The restocking fee is **waived** in two cases:

- **Store error** — wrong item, damaged item, or any mistake on our end. Never
  charge a restocking fee when the return is our fault.
- **Store credit** — when the customer takes store credit instead of a cash
  refund, no restocking fee is deducted; the full amount is issued.

If a customer disputes the fee claiming it was a store error, verify the order
first. If it genuinely was our mistake, the fee should be waived. Because this
involves a refund amount, route the money decision to a human per
`refunds-and-disputes.md`.

## How to explain the restocking fee

When a customer asks about or objects to the fee, explain it plainly and offer the
fee-free alternative: the small fee covers the payment-processor charge that
applies to every refund and cancellation per store policy. Then offer **store
credit** as the way to avoid it — store credit is issued for the full amount with
no deduction. Keep the tone helpful, not defensive. Do not confirm or process the
refund itself in an automated draft; refunds are escalation-only.
