## Customer situation

Customer received an item that doesn't match the listing or arrived defective
(e.g. a footie listed as "open feet" arrived closed-feet, or two sizes arrived
mis-sized versus each other). They include or are asked for a photo.

## How it was handled

Agent recognized this as the vendor's/our error (not a buyer return), apologized
briefly, and provided a prepaid return label link for the order. For a vendor
quality question, the agent contacted the brand and set a follow-up expectation
("follow up with me Monday"). No final-sale rule was applied.

## Ideal reply

> Hi — so sorry about that, it looks like a mistake on the manufacturer's end.
> Here's a prepaid return label for that item: [return label link]. Once it's on
> the way back we'll get the correct one out / sort out a store credit for you.

## Why this is the model

- Treats a defect as our responsibility — no final-sale language.
- Concrete next step: photo → prepaid label → replacement/credit.
- **Does not promise a refund** — any refund is escalated to a human. The
  automated tone stays at "we'll make it right" with a label and replacement.
